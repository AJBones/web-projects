A Little Bit About Me

I am a biochemistry DPhil student at the University of Oxford, based in the plant sciences department. I am working on developing sustainable fuel and chemical production using bacteria in the genus Clostridium. I am in my final year of research and am currently looking for job opportunities in research and technology.

Research Background and Skills

I have a first class BSc in Biochemistry and a MSc (Res) in Parasitology. My research for my Master's involved testing a panel of cancer cell lines for their ability to act as host cells for intracellular parasites in the genus Cryptosporidium. My DPhil research involves metabolic engineering, combining wet lab and computational approaches to improve n-butanol production in bacteria. I have experience in molecular biology research, as well as plant and mammalian tissue culture, working with anaerobic organisms and performing biochemical analysis using gas-chromatography mass-spectrometry. I have experience writing scripts and programs in Python 3, performing statistical analysis of data in R and using Git for version control.
